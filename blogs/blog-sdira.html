Using a Self-Directed IRA with Checkbook Control

Published: September 13, 2025 · Retirement · Alternative Investments · Personal Finance

Most retirement accounts offered by major brokerages limit you to traditional investments like stocks, bonds, or mutual funds. But what if you want to use your IRA to invest in real estate, private businesses, precious metals, or even cryptocurrency? That’s where a Self-Directed IRA (SDIRA) with checkbook control comes in.

What is a Self-Directed IRA?

A Self-Directed IRA is a type of retirement account that expands your investment options beyond what typical firms allow. You still get the same tax advantages of a traditional or Roth IRA, but you’re not limited to Wall Street products. Instead, you can put your retirement money into a wide range of alternative assets.

The Role of the Custodian

By law, every IRA must have a custodian. In a Self-Directed IRA, the custodian acts as a passive, third-party administrator. Their job is to hold the account, keep records, and ensure your investments follow IRS rules. Importantly, they do not make investment decisions — you do.

Checkbook Control

With checkbook control, your Self-Directed IRA is structured through an LLC. You, as the IRA owner, are the manager of that LLC. The IRA custodian holds the account, but once your IRA funds are transferred into the LLC’s bank account, you can write checks directly to make investments.

This structure gives you the freedom to act quickly on opportunities without waiting for custodian approval each time. For example, if you find a real estate property to invest in, you can write a check from your IRA-owned LLC instead of submitting multiple forms to your custodian.

My Experience with SDIRA Real Estate

Personally, I’ve used my Self-Directed IRA purely for residential rental properties. This has allowed me to diversify outside of traditional markets and create long-term, income-producing assets inside my retirement account. The rental income flows back into the IRA-owned LLC, compounding tax-deferred (or tax-free in the case of a Roth). Over time, the steady cash flow and potential appreciation of the properties have become a cornerstone of my retirement strategy.

Why Consider This Approach?

  • Expand beyond stocks and bonds into real estate, private lending, startups, and more.
  • Maintain tax-deferred (or tax-free in a Roth) growth while investing in alternative assets.
  • Gain speed and flexibility by writing checks directly for investments.
  • Stay compliant with IRS rules through the required custodian oversight.

Things to Keep in Mind

While powerful, Self-Directed IRAs with checkbook control come with responsibilities. You must avoid prohibited transactions, such as investing in something that personally benefits you or your family today, rather than your retirement. You’ll also need to carefully document and track all transactions for IRS compliance.

Final Thoughts

A Self-Directed IRA with checkbook control can be a smart way to take control of your retirement portfolio and invest in areas most traditional custodians don’t allow. My own experience with residential rental properties has proven to me that these accounts offer flexibility, growth, and steady income potential — all while keeping the tax advantages of an IRA intact.
