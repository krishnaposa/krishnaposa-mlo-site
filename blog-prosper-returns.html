My 5-Year Experience with Prosper P2P Investing

Published: August 23, 2025 · Investing · P2P Lending · Personal Finance

About five years ago, I allocated a slice of my portfolio to peer-to-peer lending on Prosper. The headline borrower rates (often 8–12% depending on grade) looked attractive and felt like a nice diversifier.

As the notes aged, late payments, defaults, servicing/collection fees, prepayments, and idle cash between allocations chipped away at the yield.

Net-net, my all-time return settled around 3.8%—well below note rates once defaults, fees, cash drag, and taxes are factored in. Not “bad,” just different from the headline APRs.

Key Takeaways

  • Defaults + servicing/collection fees are the biggest haircut.
  • Prepayments cap interest and force reinvestment at uncertain rates.
  • Idle cash between allocations hurts returns.
  • Taxed as ordinary income reduces the net further.
